The Valencia City Council and the city are the capital of the Valencian Community and the province of Valencia.
One of the main tourist destinations in Spain, an international, crowded and cosmopolitan city.

A tourist city, it has endless charms for the visitor, the avant-garde City of Arts and Sciences, one of the largest recreational and cultural complexes in Spain.

Traditional its old town, the Barrio del Carmen, its palaces, stroll through its hissing of cobblestone streets, adorned with surprising medieval buildings.

The Plaza de la Virgen with its UNESCO World Heritage Site, Museums and the town hall, some of the most representative buildings in the city.

Its beaches are synonymous with quality, it has 15 km of coastline where you can enjoy the Mediterranean, hidden and surprising, practically virgin places such as Albufera or El Saler beach, considered one of the best in Spain.

Paella, a world gastronomic reference, savoring its star dish is one of the unique pleasures of this great city.

It is also a city that is growing in conference tourism, thanks to a large number of national and international events, such as F1 in the newly created Valencia Urban Circuit, next to the port, or the America's Cup of Sailing. Furthermore, in 2010 the AVE to Madrid was inaugurated, providing it with a complete transport network.

Job

They have sought to do things differently, so an action plan of each governing council in the city is constant improvement and job creation, and through actions they have sought to bring in both national and foreign investments. These investments and actions mean that more than 80% of the city's economic activities are services, so in order to provide quality services it is necessary to have employees, so the opportunities found in Valencia are very great with the labor office to generate greater opportunities and provide guidance in this area.

Party

Festivals are big words because we are not only referring to the fact that having beaches, young people are always at sea level parties, but also traditional festivals that its Mediterranean essence has made it famous in patron saint festivals and celebrations of Valencian customs for What a common characteristic of every celebration is the joy and bustle that accompanies religious festivals and popular festivals, being that rich culture is found to give nights and days full of fun, joy and celebration to life.

Culture

In Valencia, culture is taken seriously, as evidenced by its more than 30 museums scattered throughout the city hall, so you can visit everything from modern art exhibitions to going back to the past of Valencia and the autonomous community, you can learn about modernity and the roots of the city, enjoy cultural events such as theater and concerts in halls, auditoriums and theaters as well as enjoy national and foreign exhibitions that reflect the importance of art and culture not only for the city council of Valencia. Living Valencia is living the contrast of modern and ancient, of origin and progress, which is why you should not stop living it.

Curiosities Of Valencia

Valencia (Valencian Community)

Beautiful and famous city, capital of its community, houses a multitude of magnificent curiosities inside that, sometimes, even its inhabitants are unaware of. Surprisingly, Valencia is considered the city with the most traffic lights per inhabitant in Europe and the second in the world (the first is New York). Without a doubt, Valencian traffic has to work like clockwork.

Valencia (Valencian Community)

The Cathedral-Basilica Church of Our Lady of Valencia was built on the old Balansiya mosque, which had previously stood on the old Visigothic cathedral. In it, since 1424, a small cup has been kept, to which jewels and a gold structure have been added. Some experts in the field consider it to be the Holy Grail.
